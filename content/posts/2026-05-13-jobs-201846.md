The Jeju job market is experiencing a notable upward trend, with employment rates rising and unemployment rates declining. According to the latest report from the National Data Center's Jeju office, the employment rate in the region reached 71.7% last month, marking a 2.5 percentage point increase compared to the same month last year.

In terms of job statistics, the number of employed individuals rose to 414,000, an increase of 14,000 from the previous year. Interestingly, while temporary workers saw a significant increase of 19.5%, there was a decline in the number of permanent and daily workers. This shift indicates a dynamic change in the employment landscape, with self-employed individuals also showing growth.

As Jeju continues to evolve economically, it will be fascinating to see how these trends impact the local community and what new opportunities may arise. Are we witnessing the beginning of a new era for Jeju's workforce?
