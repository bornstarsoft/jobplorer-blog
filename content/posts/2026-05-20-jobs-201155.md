The global accounting industry is undergoing a seismic shift as firms like Deloitte, EY, KPMG, and PwC invest billions into AI development. With AI-related job postings expected to surpass traditional audit roles by 2025, the landscape for new graduates is changing dramatically. Recent data indicates that AI positions now account for about 7% of job listings in English-speaking countries, a significant increase from less than 2% in 2022.

However, this investment in technology comes with a downside: a sharp decline in entry-level hiring. For instance, KPMG has reduced its new graduate recruitment by 29% in 2023, while other firms are following suit. This trend reflects a broader move towards automation and outsourcing, raising questions about the future of entry-level positions in the industry.

Despite these challenges, there are emerging opportunities in AI audit services, which are being rapidly developed to ensure compliance and performance of AI models. As firms adapt to these changes, the focus is shifting towards enhancing the skills of existing employees rather than expanding the workforce. Will the accounting profession be able to balance the benefits of AI with the need for human talent in the years to come?
