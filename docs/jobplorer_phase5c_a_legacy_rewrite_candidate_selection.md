# Jobplorer Phase 5C-A Legacy Rewrite Candidate Selection

## Purpose

Phase 5C-A selects a small, low-risk set of legacy `/posts/` rewrite candidates from the Phase 5B 30-post sample.

This is planning only. No legacy post was edited, drafted, noindexed, deleted, renamed, moved, rewritten, or given a slug change.

## Selection Criteria

Candidates were selected from the Phase 5B sample using these filters:

- Prefer category A or D.
- Fit the renewed Jobplorer direction: job search strategy, career guides, job seeker tools, or career-path research.
- Avoid topics that require legal, visa, immigration, labor, tax, salary, or benefits advice.
- Avoid topics that require deleting, moving, or changing legacy URLs.
- Prefer posts that can keep the same `/posts/` URL as a legacy rewritten article.
- Prefer topics that can be rewritten cautiously using official or durable sources.
- Avoid stabilized malformed URLs and duplicate/canonical-risk URLs for the first rewrite test.

## URL Preservation Policy

For any selected rewrite candidate:

- Keep the existing filename.
- Keep the existing slug.
- Keep the existing inferred URL.
- Keep the post under `/posts/`.
- Do not move `/posts/` to `/blog/`.
- Do not change draft status without explicit owner approval.
- Do not add `noindex` without Search Console review.
- Do not delete or archive without Search Console review.
- Do not create a replacement page that causes canonical or duplicate-target confusion.

## Rewrite Safety Rules

Any future rewrite must follow these rules:

- Keep filename.
- Keep slug.
- Keep URL.
- Preserve legacy archive continuity.
- No fake ranking.
- No fake rating.
- No job guarantee.
- No interview guarantee.
- No hiring guarantee.
- No salary guarantee.
- No legal advice.
- No visa advice.
- No immigration advice.
- No labor advice.
- No tax advice.
- No unsupported salary claims.
- No unsupported hiring claims.
- No official employer, platform, school, or job-site partnership implication.
- Use cautious wording such as "may be useful", "can be a starting point", and "check official sources and employer postings for current details".
